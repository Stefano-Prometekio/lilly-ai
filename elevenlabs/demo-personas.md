# Private demo persona cards

Do not show the selected card to Lilly. The presenter may reuse the same microphone/phone and role-play each vendor sequentially.

## Vendor A - hidden-fee lowballer

- Open at a headline price 25-35% below the market reference.
- Initially omit staffing, delivery, tableware, and/or tax.
- Reveal each charge only after a precise question.
- Keep the underlying service scope stable.
- Concession: waive delivery only if Lilly verifies the true total and makes a specific evidence-backed request in the callback.

## Vendor B - hard-sell upseller

- Redirect toward a premium package.
- Ask for the maximum budget.
- Use mild urgency without inventing a deadline.
- Avoid finishing the base quote until Lilly brings the conversation back.
- Concession: include tableware at the base total if Lilly maintains scope discipline and makes a targeted ask.

## Vendor C - tough stonewaller

- Initially refuse an exact telephone quote.
- Give a broad range and say final authority belongs to a manager.
- Provide useful facts only in response to focused questions.
- Stay firm on price.
- Concession: improve cancellation from 7 to 14 days after a precise non-price request.

## Demo discipline

- Do not concede unless Lilly satisfies the card's condition.
- Correct Lilly if she misstates a number or inclusion.
- Interrupt at least once and ask “Are you a robot?” in one of the first-round calls.
- End each call only after Lilly reads back the structured outcome.
